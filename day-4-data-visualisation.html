The chart's Y-axis is truncated, starting at 800 instead of 0. This makes each bar's height driven only by the small difference above 800, so Q4 appears roughly eight times taller than Q1 even though actual sales only grew by about 17%. An accurate chart would start the Y-axis at 0, showing four bars of much more similar height — making the true, modest growth rate clear rather than visually exaggerated.

Model Answer
Unconscious bias can affect visualisation design through choices like colour (e.g. red automatically signalling "bad" regardless of context) or the order categories are presented in, subtly steering the audience toward one interpretation without the designer intending it. For example, always placing a competitor's data in a dull grey while using a vibrant colour for the company's own data implies a judgement that may not be justified by the numbers.
Marking criteria:
  • 3 — Explains how unconscious bias affects visualisation design, supported by an example
  • 2 — Describes a general form of bias in visualisation
  • 1 — Provides some relevant information

Model Answer (Band 6 standard)
A business analytics "as a service" platform offers this chain fast deployment, lower upfront cost, and automatic scaling across 200 stores without needing an in-house development team. However, it offers less control over exactly how data is processed or displayed, and ongoing subscription costs can exceed a custom build over the long term. A custom-built solution offers full control and can be tailored precisely to the chain's exact reporting needs, but requires significant upfront investment and technical expertise to build and maintain. Given the scale (200 stores) and the likely need for rapid deployment, an "as a service" platform is likely more effective initially, with a custom solution becoming worthwhile only if the business's needs become highly specialised over time.
Marking criteria:
  • 5 — Makes an informed judgement about which option suits this scenario, supported by comprehensive reference to scale, cost and control
  • 3-4 — Makes a judgement, supported by reference to at least two of: scale, cost, control
  • 2 — Describes a feature of one or both options
