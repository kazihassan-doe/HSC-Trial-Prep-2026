Blockchain can store each ticket's ownership record in a decentralised, immutable ledger. Once issued, the ticket's data cannot be altered without consensus across the network, so any resale or transfer is recorded transparently. Enterprises must respect this by ensuring collection is consensual and culturally appropriate, and by not assuming ownership of that data themselves — avoiding historical patterns of data misuse.

Model Answer (Band 6 standard)
This approach raises significant ethical and legal issues. A pre-ticked checkbox does not constitute genuine informed consent, since most customers will not read lengthy terms of agreement before proceeding, undermining their right to control their own data. This could breach privacy legislation requiring clear, affirmative consent for data collection. Ethically, it exploits user inattention to gain broad data reuse rights, risking dishonest practice and reduced trust. To be defensible, the company should require an unticked, clearly worded consent option and communicate data use transparently rather than legally minimally.
Marking criteria:
  • 5 — Makes an informed judgement about the ethical/legal issues, supported by comprehensive reference to consent, privacy expectations and enterprise responsibility
  • 3-4 — Makes a judgement about the ethical/legal issues, supported by reference to consent and/or privacy expectations
