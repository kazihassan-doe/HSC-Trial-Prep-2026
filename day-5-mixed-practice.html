Model Answer
Key fields (primary and foreign keys) allow related data to be stored once in one table and referenced from other tables, rather than repeating the same information across multiple records. For example, a customer's details are stored once in a Customers table and linked to multiple orders via a foreign key, instead of re-entering their details on every order.

Model Answer
When a chart's Y-axis doesn't start at zero, small real differences between values appear visually exaggerated, because bar heights are driven only by the range above the truncation point. This can lead an audience to believe growth or change is far more dramatic than the underlying data actually shows.

Model Answer (Band 6 standard)
An average alone can be misleading, since a single unusually high or low day (an outlier) can distort the figure and mask genuine day-to-day patterns — such as consistently low weekday activity offset by one very active weekend day. This means the average may not accurately reflect the user's typical behaviour, reducing the dashboard's real usefulness for tracking meaningful progress. A more effective visualisation would show the full daily trend (e.g. a line graph across the week) alongside the average, letting users see both the overall pattern and any unusual outliers — giving a more reliable and actionable picture than the average alone.
Marking criteria:
  • 4 — Makes an informed judgement about the dashboard's reliability, supported by comprehensive reference to bias/outliers, and proposes why an alternative would be more effective
  • 2-3 — Makes a judgement, supported by some reference to bias, outliers or effectiveness
  • 1 — Provides some relevant information
